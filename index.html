About the Artist

Early life

Clemente Spampinato was born in Calabria, Italy in 1912. At a young age while being schooled in the classical arts in Rome, he developed a keen love for both art and athletics. He became a devoted follower of top Italian sporting events. He befriended many of Italy's top-flight athletes including the great Olympic Champions. In the 1930's & 1940's he was commissioned to sculpt many Bronze Trophies & Medallions for the Olympics and other Italian sporting events.

His work

His sculpture captures the "essence of movement" beautifully when the human body is at the "greatest point of height, speed and dynamics". His belief that art, in particular sculpture, has its objective solidly rooted in tradition. He has been admired for his ability to bring to life the complex movements of sport and western life while at the same time maintaining a strong sense of realism. Each bronze sculpture is cast in special limited editions and is handcrafted in the "lost wax" process.

His accompli

Clemente Spampinato's bronzes are displayed in many museums, galleries and both private and public collections throughout the United States, Canada and Europe. Currently, some of his sculptures can be found at the following locations: Clydes of Georgetown, Washington DC; Clydes of Gallery Place, Washington DC; National Baseball Hall of Fame, Coopertown, NY; Pro Football Hall of Fame, Canton, OH; Vintage 429 Landmark Gallery, New Orleans, LA. The complete sculpture collection is on display at the Redwood Gallery in Sea Cliff, NY.

Place an order

Sport and Western Collection sculptures range in prices starting from $3,000 to $15,000 and up for each Sport bronze, and $5,800 to $21,000 and up for each Western bronze, depending on size and subject. Many bronze sculptures are available for immediate delivery while others must be ordered. All bronzes can be custom ordered in various dimensions and a variety of patinas.

Each sculpture is created using the “Lost Wax” process whereby each piece is created as a clay armature then a plaster mold is made that eventually creates a wax mold and finally the sculpture is cast into bronze. Therefore custom order pieces may take three to five months depending on size and finish. Each bronze is individually created, whereby patina and sizes are approximate and may vary slightly.

Virtually any bronze in the collection can be enlarged or reduced.
